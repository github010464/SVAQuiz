  • Singular subjects take singular verbs; plural subjects take plural verbs.
    • She walks. / They walk.
  • When two subjects are joined by "and," use a plural verb.
    • Tom and Jerry are funny.
  • When two singular subjects are joined by "or" or "nor," use a singular verb.
    • Neither John nor Lisa is coming.
  • If one subject is singular and the other plural, the verb agrees with the nearer subject (in "or/nor" situations).
    • Neither the teachers nor the student has the book.
  • Indefinite pronouns (e.g., everyone, someone, each) take singular verbs.
    • Everyone is ready.
  • Collective nouns usually take singular verbs (unless emphasizing individuals).
    • The team wins every match.
    • The team are arguing among themselves.
  • Titles of books, movies, or countries take singular verbs.
    • The United States is a big country.
  • Amounts of time, money, distance, or weight usually take singular verbs.
    • Five dollars is not enough.
